NEW EXPRESSIONS

What are you doing?

A question using the present continuous structure to ask a question about the future. That structure can be used in affirmatives, negatives, and interrogative sentences, and its meaning will vary depending on the context.

When are you leaving?

I’m not working tomorrow.

She’s going out tonight

Make excuses

An expression that means to seek to lessen blame, try to justify, coming up with unjustifiable reasons to justify blame.

I know you were lying, stop making excuses.

She had to make excuses for being late again.

Oddball

And adjective meaning a strange or eccentric person.

She was painting with her hair, like the oddball that she is.

We’re considered oddballs by our neighbors.
